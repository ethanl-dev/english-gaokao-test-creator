# Museum Offers Touch Tours for Blind Visitors

**Source:** Sample News

**URL:** https://example.com/museum-touch-tours

**Published:** 2026-06-22

The City History Museum has long been a place where visitors look at paintings, maps, and ancient tools behind glass. Starting next month, however, blind and low-vision guests will be able to experience the collection in a new way. The museum is launching guided touch tours that allow participants to handle carefully selected objects.

"Touch is a powerful way to learn," said Grace Lopez, the museum's education director. "When someone holds a century-old tool or feels the surface of a sculpture, history becomes real."

The tours were developed with help from a local organization that supports people with visual disabilities. Trained guides describe each object in detail and explain its historical importance before letting visitors touch it. The museum has also created large-print labels and audio descriptions for several permanent exhibitions.

Although only twenty objects can be touched during each tour, the staff hopes to add more in the future. They are also planning special family days so that children with visual impairments can visit with their parents.

Critics have raised concerns about protecting fragile items. Museum officials say every object on the tour has been examined by conservators and that visitors wear gloves. "Accessibility and preservation can work together," Lopez said. "Our goal is to make sure everyone has the chance to connect with the past."

The first touch tour will take place on July 8, and tickets are already selling quickly.
